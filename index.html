Program Management ScoreCard

Indicator# Indicator Descriptions and Notes
- Indicators 1 - 4 are quantitative and should be extracted from Salesforce, these can be populated by anyone.
- Indicators 5 - 6 are subjective and should be extracted from the Pre-Sales Analysis sheet which contains Data, these can be populated by anyone.
- Indicator 7 is subjective should be populated by the Program Management Team.
1 Starting MRR for a WorkStream or $ value for a Pilot, OTP, or POC. This data is extracted from Salesforce.
2 From company firmographics, aligned with Company's Sales definition. This data is extracted from Salesforce.
3 Sales AE and/or CS opinion on expansion potential. This data is extracted from Salesforce.
4 Contract type: Pilot, OTP, POC, or Subscription. This data is extracted from Salesforce.
5 Defined by Pre Sales Team during analysis - Easy: Learning curve less than 1 week, Medium: Learning curve 1-2 weeks, Hard: Learning curve 2-4 weeks.
6 Defined by Pre Sales during analysis - Easy: Learning curve less than 1 week, Medium: Learning curve 1-2 weeks, Hard: Learning curve 2-4 weeks.
7 The level of leadership (PM/TL) focus required due to complexities in the processes, workflow, or team management. This indicator is controlled and populated by the Program Management Team
Definitions - POC: Proof of Concept is a small sprint executed by a small Core Team to show that Company can do the task.
- Pilot: A short term project that involves onboarding a team of Workers who will execute a set number of tasks and prove that Company can do the task.
- OTP: A one-time-project is a finite project that involves a team of Workers completing a set number of tasks within a set time frame.
- Subscription: An ongoing project that will have a target number of hours to deliver to the Client on a monthly basis.
This scorecard should be used as a guide before requesting PM resources. All requests should be submitted to the Head of Program Management for review.
